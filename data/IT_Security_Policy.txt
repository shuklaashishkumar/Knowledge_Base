Article Title: IT Security Policy
Category: IT / Compliance
Last Updated: August 26, 2025

---------------------------------
Overview
---------------------------------
This article defines the IT Security Policy at Learning Thoughts. It establishes guidelines to protect company systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, misuse, and security threats.

---------------------------------
1. Purpose
---------------------------------
- Ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of company information.
- Protect employees, customers, and partners from cyber threats.
- Define employee responsibilities for maintaining IT security.

---------------------------------
2. Scope
---------------------------------
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, interns, and third parties who have access to Learning Thoughtsâ€™ IT systems, data, and infrastructure.

---------------------------------
3. Access Control
---------------------------------
- Employees must use unique credentials for all systems and platforms.
- Strong passwords (minimum 8 characters with complexity) must be used.
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is mandatory for critical systems.
- Access rights are assigned based on job role (principle of least privilege).

---------------------------------
4. Data Protection & Privacy
---------------------------------
- Sensitive company and customer data must be encrypted in storage and transit.
- Personal Identifiable Information (PII) must be handled as per privacy laws.
- Employees must not copy company data to personal devices without approval.
- Data disposal must follow secure deletion practices.

---------------------------------
5. Device & Network Security
---------------------------------
- Company laptops and devices must have up-to-date antivirus and security patches.
- Employees must not install unauthorized software or applications.
- Public Wi-Fi should be avoided for official work; use VPN when necessary.
- USB drives and external devices require IT approval before use.

---------------------------------
6. Email & Communication Security
---------------------------------
- Do not open suspicious emails, links, or attachments.
- Company email must not be used for personal communications.
- Confidential information should only be shared through secure channels.
- Phishing or social engineering attempts must be reported immediately.

---------------------------------
7. Incident Reporting
---------------------------------
- All security incidents (data breach, malware infection, suspicious login) must be reported to IT immediately.
- The IT team will investigate and take corrective measures.
- Employees must cooperate during incident response activities.

---------------------------------
8. Remote Work Security
---------------------------------
- Remote employees must use secure VPN connections.
- Devices must be password-protected and auto-lock enabled.
- Company-approved collaboration tools must be used for work communication.
- Employees must not share company data over personal email or messaging apps.

---------------------------------
9. Compliance & Disciplinary Actions
---------------------------------
- Violations of this policy may lead to disciplinary action, including termination.
- Non-compliance with legal and regulatory IT requirements may result in penalties.
- Regular IT security audits ensure compliance and identify vulnerabilities.

---------------------------------
Troubleshooting / FAQs
---------------------------------
Q1: What should I do if I forget my password?
A1: Contact the IT Helpdesk to reset your password securely.

Q2: How do I know if an email is a phishing attempt?
A2: Look for suspicious links, spelling errors, or requests for sensitive data. When in doubt, report to IT.

Q3: Can I use my personal laptop for work?
A3: Only if approved by IT and with proper security configurations.
